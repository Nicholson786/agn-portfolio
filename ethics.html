A code of ethics written for a doctoral program
could be an academic exercise.

This one is not.

Every principle on this page has a corresponding record — a team led, a system built, a standard held under pressure, a community served. This code was not written to describe who I intend to become. It was written to formalize who I already am.

"My cumulative purpose is to lead with integrity, elevate others, and ensure that my influence contributes to a culture of trust, accountability, and sustainable performance."

// CORE LEADERSHIP VALUES
FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES
[ INTEGRITY ]

I will lead with honesty, transparency, and consistency. My decisions will be set firmly upon a foundation of truth, even when the truth is inconvenient or costly.

[ RESPECT FOR PEOPLE ]

I will recognize the dignity of every individual. I will create environments where people feel valued, heard, and empowered to make their contributions.

[ ACCOUNTABILITY ]

I will take ownership of the outcomes of my decisions and the culture I am responsible for helping to create. I will openly claim responsibility for my mistakes and will take actions to correct them.

[ JUSTICE & FAIRNESS ]

I will strive for equal treatment, decision-making without bias, and ethical consistency across all levels of the organization.

[ EXCELLENCE & CI ]

I will pursue disciplined execution, strict operational rigor, and a commitment to ongoing learning. I expect the same commitment from myself that I ask of others.

[ STEWARDSHIP ]

I will act as a responsible steward of organizational resources, people, and trust. My leadership must leave systems stronger than I found them.

// PROFESSIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES
OBLIGATIONS ON RECORD
TO THE ORGANIZATION
  • Uphold ethical standards, even under pressure to compromise
  • Promote a culture of safety, quality, and continuous improvement
  • Protect confidential information and organizational assets
  • Challenge decisions or behaviors that undermine integrity or long-term sustainability
TO EMPLOYEES & TEAMS
  • Model the behaviors expected of others
  • Provide clarity, coaching, and support to help individuals grow
  • Ensure fairness in opportunities, workload, and recognition
  • Build psychological safety so employees can speak up, innovate, and learn
TO COLLEAGUES & PARTNERS
  • Collaborate with professionalism and respect
  • Communicate openly, directly, and constructively
  • Address conflict with maturity and commitment to shared goals
  • Support alignment between departments to strengthen organizational performance
TO CLIENTS, CUSTOMERS & THE PUBLIC
  • Deliver reliable, quality work that reflects the organization's values
  • Consider the broader societal and environmental impact of decisions
  • Represent the organization with professionalism and honesty
// DECISION PROTOCOL
ETHICAL DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK
ETHICAL DECISION PROTOCOL // ACTIVE
STEP 01
Define the issue. Identify all stakeholders.
STEP 02
Examine relevant values, policies, and professional standards.
STEP 03
Evaluate options using moral reasoning — consider both immediate and long-term impacts.
STEP 04
Seek counsel from trusted mentors, leaders, or ethical resources when uncertainty exists.
STEP 05
Act with integrity. Choose the option that best aligns with ethical principles and respect for people.
STEP 06
Reflect on the outcome to strengthen future judgment and leadership maturity.
